I am a mathematician at the <a href="https://www.mimuw.edu.pl/en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Faculty of Mathematics, Informatics and Mechanics</a> of the University of Warsaw. Starting from October 2026 I will hold there the position of Samuel Eilenberg Assistant Professor. Currently, I am a postdoc working in two research projects: one led by <a href="https://www.mimuw.edu.pl/~bmia/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Błażej Miasojedow</a> and the other by <a href="https://www.mimuw.edu.pl/~ichlebicka/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Iwona Chlebicka</a>.

I completed my PhD in Warsaw, advised by <a href="https://www.il.pw.edu.pl/en/member/lewinski-tomasz-en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Tomasz Lewiński</a> and <a href="https://www.mimuw.edu.pl/~rybka/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Piotr Rybka</a>. <a href="https://theses.hal.science/tel-05609664v1" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">My thesis</a> concerned the optimal design of engineering structures. My mathematical perspective on this topic has been shaped by the works of <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/gbouchitte/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Guy Bouchitté</a>, a dear collaborator of mine ever since before my defense.

In my dissertation, I touched the Monge-Kantorovich optimal transport problem. I had the opportunity to pursue this research direction during my 2-year postdoc at the Lagrange Mathematics and Computing Research Center in Paris, where I was a part of the project led by <a href="https://www.ceremade.dauphine.fr/~carlier/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Guillaume Carlier</a>, <a href="https://people.math.ethz.ch/~afigalli/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Alessio Figalli</a>, <a href="http://quentin.mrgt.fr/#teaching" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Quentin Mérigot</a>, and <a href="https://math.univ-lyon1.fr/~santambrogio/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Filippo Santambrogio</a>.

My path started not with mathematics but engineering. I have studied at the <a href="https://www.il.pw.edu.pl/en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Faculty of Civil Engineering of the Warsaw University of Technology</a> and continued there for several years as a teaching/research assistant. In that period, I was lucky to have <a href="https://www.il.pw.edu.pl/en/member/lewinski-tomasz-en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Tomasz Lewiński</a> for a mentor. It was his exceptional approach to the theory of structural mechanics that sparked my inclination toward mathematical rigour. Today, mechanical problems continue to be my source of inspiration for new mathematics.
